# MinionMint Product Doctrine Guardrail

MinionMint mints bounded AI Minions through a guided blueprint process. Do not implement it as a generic chatbot builder, prompt manager, or widget generator.

Working foundation:

- The first user experience is a minting interview.
- The output is a Minion Blueprint, not a prompt, widget, or generic assistant profile.
- A Minion is a bounded, approval-gated operating identity with a mission, knowledge vault, memory rules, approval rails, planned communication identity, planned app access, owner-controlled credentials, observability, and future workspace plan.
- Future Minions may have AgentPhone-style phone or SMS identity, AgentMail-style email identity, AgentCard-style controlled payment identity, Composio-style app connections, cloud-computer-style workspace access, Hermes plus Obsidian or HermesVault knowledge, and Latitude-style traces.
- These identity surfaces are planned in Phase 1 only. Do not enable live phone, email, payment, app, credential, or computer-use integrations unless explicitly approved.
- Keep Phase 2 paused until Phase 1 is production-real enough with auth, durable persistence, model-backed blueprint generation, editing, refinement, approval, and operations-board review.
- Use customer-facing MinionMint language: Minion, Minions, Minion Blueprint, minting interview, mission, approval rails, operating knowledge, memory rules, knowledge vault, Minion operations board, owner takeover, and future workspace.
- Avoid generic chatbot language and broad autonomy. Do not lead with virtual computers, swarms, or computer-use before the Phase 1 blueprint contract is real.
